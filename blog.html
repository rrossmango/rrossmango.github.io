Rules to Live By, 90% of the Time

Do without saying, don't say without doing. Underpromise, overdeliver. Don't mention you are working on something until you are confident it can be finished.
You will often feel scared of starting a new challenge, or worried you won't do it properly. Stare directly at the problem until you can move onto the next one.
The three best things you can be are honest, reliable, and decisive.
Throw your phone into your backpack, into another room, just out of sight. The best way to be not distracted is to remove all distractions.
The hardest part is getting started. Once you get started, you won't want to stop.
Start early and start often.
Read a chapter every night before bed. Helps your eyes and headaches.
Control what you can control.
Stick to deadlines that you give. It's worse to admit you have nothing new to show than to provide some sort of update, anyway.
Never text something that may start a fight.
The bold idiot beats out the smart overthinker in the end.
Just turn your brain off and start. Don't worry about the leg work, don't worry about chasing perfection.
